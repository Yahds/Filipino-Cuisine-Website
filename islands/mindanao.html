Biryani

It is said that the Muslim population in the Indian/Persian area produces the meal known as biryani. Thus, it is not surprising that this delectable recipe, which is a staple of Mindanao cuisine, has reached the Muslim community. A meal called "biryani" is essentially fried rice with chunks of chicken or fish and other seasonings.

Curacha

In Mindanao, particularly in Zamboanga, curacha is well-known. The hybrid crustacean, known as a spanner crab or red frog crab, is an inhabitants of the seas of Zamboanga and Sulu. It may be steamed or cooked with sauce.

Pastil

Popular Mindanao cuisine called pastil could be found in Maguindanao's streets. It's simply rice wrapped in a banana leaf with slices of beef or chicken adobo within. The dish is also available in Zamboanga. Their pastil is similar to an empanada, except the contents are pancit noodles (rice noodles) rather than meat and veggies.

Pyanggang

Pyanggang is a Tausug delicacy that resembles grilled chicken, only it's black rather than burned. Coconut flesh, one of the components, is burned before it is pulverized. It is then added to additional spices to create a paste or sauce for the chicken's marinade.
