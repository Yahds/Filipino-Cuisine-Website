Luzon Cuisine

Food from the island of Luzon in the Philippines is a vibrant and diverse representation of Filipino cuisine. Luzon, being the largest and most populous island in the country, is home to a wide range of culinary traditions that reflect its cultural and geographical diversity. Food from Luzon, Philippines, is a reflection of the island's rich cultural heritage, incorporating a blend of indigenous, Spanish, and Asian influences. The diversity of ingredients and cooking methods make Luzon's cuisine a delightful exploration of flavors, textures, and traditions.

Did you know?

Kakanin are local rice snacks found all over Luzon. However due to modernization, the traditionally methods and ingredients in making kakanin is slowly fading away (De Villa et al., 2022).

Sisig

Photo by junpinzon on depositphotos

Sisig is a dish that originated from Pampanga. The dish's name stems from the old Tagalog word “Sisigan”, meaning “to make sour” (Gilbuena, 2023). It is made with chopped pork, onions and chilies. However, sisig used to be a simple salad, check this article to learn more about the dish’s origins.

Kapampangan Kare kare

Photo by Mia Malantic on Pinterest

Kare kare is a peanut based stew spiced with annatto powder and filled with oxtail/beef, eggplant, pechay, string beans, and more.

Bulalo

Photo by Foxy Folksy on Pinterest

Bulalo is a beef dish popular for having a beef shank and bone marrow all leading to a tasty broth. It is traditionally paired with vegetables such as cabbage, pechay, corn, and etc.

Pinakbet Ilocano

Photo by Mama’s Guide Recipes on Pinterest

Pinakbet is a vegetable dish as it is composed of a variety of vegetables with fermented fish, “bagoong isda” added to it in the Ilocano version of the dish. Fried pork belly is traditionally added to provide protein in the meal.

Lechon Kawali

Photo by Derek Chen on instagram

Lechon kawali is fried pork belly cut into small pieced, usually mixed with sauce when served.

Pork Igado

Photo by May Oller on Pinterest

Pork Igado is an Ilocano stew made of liver and port tenderloin braised in a soy sauce-vinegar mixture and spices such as garlic, onions, and bay leaves. Common additions to the dish are other innards such as kindey or heart, and green peas, bell peppers.

Tupig

Photo by thel011mnl on Pinterest

Tupig is a delicacy from Pangasinan made by frying a mixture of glutinous rice, shredded young coconut, coconut milk, sugar, and butter after wrapping it in banana leaves.

Papaitan

Photo by Dem Pilar on Pinterest

Papaitan is an Ilocano soup dish composed of cow or goat innards. The dish’s name is derived from the Filipino word “Pait”, meaning “bitter”. The soup is better because of the bile. Check this article to know more about the dish.

Crispy Pata

Photo by junpinzon on depositphotos

Crispy Pata is a dish made from pork leg. It is deep-fried or can be put in the oven; however, the main target is for the dish to have a crispy skin. To learn more about the dish check out this article.

Tibok-tibok

Photo by John Allain on Pinterest

Tibok-tibok is a Kapampangan delicacy made of fresh carabao’s mild, rice flour, and latik topping. It is a mild pudding with a smooth, cream flavor and texture with a delicious hint of coconut aroma. With this, it is enjoyed as a dessert or snack.
